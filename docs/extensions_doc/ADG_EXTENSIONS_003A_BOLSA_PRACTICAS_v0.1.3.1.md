## 0. Purpose

This document defines the first child lane of Oportunidades ADG.

It defines:
- internship/junior/talent opportunity lane
- service spirit
- who it protects
- who may submit opportunities
- how ADG review works
- MVP and future data contract boundaries

---

## 1. Lane identity

Visible name:
- Bolsa de Prácticas / Juniors / Talents

Type:
- service
- opportunity layer
- entry-to-sector support lane

Current state:
- No existe nada

---

## 2. Main role

The lane should become:
- a service of opportunities for entry into the design sector
- initially focused on internships and junior roles
- connected to students, talents, and juniors as one early-career audience

It is not:
- generic job board
- free posting wall
- spam board
- school-scraping or student-scalping mechanism

Doctrine:
**This lane exists to support entry into the sector, not to exploit early-career people.**

---

## 3. Product spirit

Spirit:
- ADG community service
- curated
- protective
- institutional enough to trust
- not LinkedIn
- not InfoJobs
- not unmoderated board

Value comes from ADG review and criteria.

---

## 4. Publication model

Input may originate from:
- studios
- schools
- agencies
- relevant entities

But:
- publication passes internally through ADG
- organizations contact ADG by email
- ADG evaluates criteria
- only then can the opportunity be published

For this lane:
- intake should require deliberate contact and some effort
- avoid easy open form as first/default path

---

## 5. Audience

Audience:
- students
- talents
- junior profiles
- schools as observers/supporters

Risk:
- avoid school scalping
- avoid harvesting students/talents as supply pool

---

## 6. Public inactive state

If the lane does not exist yet:
- it may appear as grey disabled stub in hub/home
- it should not imply working application flow
- it should not be activated until ready

---

## 7. MVP technology

No MVP technology is locked.

Not locked:
- Google Forms
- email-only
- Airtable
- database
- Excel/Sheets
- custom form

Must be ready for:
- database integration
- intake tracking
- Excel/Sheets workflows
- future forms
- review statuses
- manual approval
- publication state

Rule:
**Process and data contract first; tool choice later.**

---

## 8. Opportunity types

Initial:
- internships
- junior roles
- scholarships / trainee roles

Later:
- other entry-level opportunities if aligned with criteria

---

## 9. Minimum offer fields

Minimum data:
- title
- studio/organization
- location
- modality
- duration
- remuneration
- description
- requirements
- contact/application method
- deadline
- ADG review state

Conditions and remuneration must be explicit enough to protect candidates.

---

## 10. Moderation

ADG reviews before publication.

No:
- direct public posting
- automatic publication from intake
- unverified publication flow

The review step is product value.

---

## 11. Relationship to Directorio

Future:
- offer can link to studio/agency profile if reliable
- member profile can show active opportunities later
- no fake linking if matching is unreliable

---

## 12. MVP boundary

MVP includes:
- clear public explanation
- curated opportunity list
- ADG-reviewed postings
- manual/semi-manual intake
- status fields
- basic filtering/search
- transparent conditions
- deadline and application path

MVP does not include:
- automatic self-publication
- account system
- applicant tracking
- instant applications
- open posting by anyone
- full directory/profile integration unless reliable
- freelance/professional lanes mixed into same list without separation

---

## 13. Strong version boundary

Strong version may include:
- structured intake form
- admin review workflow
- profile linking
- school/talent ecosystem signals
- better search/filtering
- expired/archived opportunities
- moderation logs
- ADG reporting dashboard
- database-backed opportunity state
- connection to Oportunidades ADG sub-home

---

## 14. Risks

- predatory internships
- unpaid or unclear offers
- spam
- school scalping
- generic job-board drift
- publication too easy
- weak ADG review UX
- mixing lanes too early
- choosing intake tooling too early
